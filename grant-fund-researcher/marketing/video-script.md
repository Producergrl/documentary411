# Setup Video Script — Grant and Fund Researcher

Target length: about 2 minutes. Record the screen the whole way through;
your voice carries it. Loom is perfect for this. One take is fine —
buyers trust a slightly imperfect real walkthrough more than a polished ad.

Before recording, have ready: a Gumroad test receipt open in one tab,
claude.ai open in another, and Gmail in a third.

---

**SCENE 1 — Your Gumroad receipt on screen**

> "You've just bought Grant and Fund Researcher. I'm going to walk you
> from this receipt to your first funding letters — the whole thing takes
> about five minutes, and you don't need to be technical at all."

**SCENE 2 — Click Download in the receipt**

> "First, click Download in your receipt. You'll get a single zip file.
> Don't unzip it or open it — Claude wants it exactly as it is."

**SCENE 3 — Go to claude.ai → Settings → Skills** (or type
claude.ai/customize/skills in the address bar on camera)

> "Now go to claude.ai, open Settings, and click Skills. Click Upload
> skill, choose the zip you just downloaded, and you're done. That's the
> entire installation."

**SCENE 4 — Settings → Connectors → Gmail → Connect**

> "One requirement: Claude needs permission to create drafts in your
> Gmail. Still in Settings, open Connectors, find Gmail, and click
> Connect — you approve it with your normal Google login. Important:
> Claude can only create drafts for you to review. It never sends
> anything. Ever."

**SCENE 5 — Open a new chat, type: "Set up Grant and Fund Researcher"**

> "Now open a new conversation and type: Set up Grant and Fund
> Researcher. Claude will interview you about your project — what it is,
> what you're raising, how you want your letters to sound. Just answer in
> plain English, like you're telling a friend."

**SCENE 6 — The live demo (let this play out on camera — it's the money shot)**

> "Here's my favorite part. Before it schedules anything, Claude proves
> it works right in front of you. It finds two or three real funders that
> fit your project, logs them in your new spreadsheet with verified
> contacts and sources, and drafts one real letter of inquiry into your
> Gmail. Open the draft and read it. If the tone isn't you, say so —
> Claude fixes it before anything goes live."

**SCENE 7 — Claude's final confirmation message on screen, then cut to
Gmail drafts with the project label visible**

> "And that's setup. Every night while you sleep, it researches new
> funders, verifies every contact, and drafts personalized letters.
> Every morning they're waiting in your Gmail drafts under your
> project's label — you review, edit, and hit send on the ones you want."

**SCENE 8 — Back to your face or the Claude chat**

> "Want to pause it, change the run time, or shut it down? Just ask
> Claude, in any conversation, any time. That's it — happy funding."

---

## Recording notes

- **Loom is the right tool.** Free tier covers this length, gives you an
  instant shareable link for the Gumroad page and receipt, and shows your
  camera bubble — a face on screen builds trust for a $29–49 purchase.
  Only upgrade path worth considering: Screen Studio (Mac) if you ever
  want the auto-zoom polished look, but don't let that delay shipping.
- Do a real setup on a real project during the recording. Cut nothing —
  if Claude takes 20 seconds to build the spreadsheet, trim it in Loom
  after.
- Put the finished video link in three places: the Gumroad product page,
  the Gumroad receipt custom message, and the README in the zip.
