A game. Not a lecture.

Every round drops players into a realistic "sitch" — with three possible moves and a wildcard.

1

Draw a card

Each card presents a real situation — peer pressure, online safety, gut instincts, body autonomy, secrets, or social pressure.

2

Choose your move

Three options per card. Or play the wildcard: "None of the above — I'm going to wow you with my answer." Convince the table and earn double points.

3

Defend your decision

Make your case. The other players decide. A thirty-second round can become an unforgettable conversation — and a lesson remembered when it matters.

4

Discover who they really are

Parents discover how their children assess risk under pressure. Kids sharpen their instincts and challenge the adults. Everyone wins.
